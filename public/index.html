Hacking is the mindset of taking an idea and building it into a prototype. We think it's essential. That's why we're hosting Learn to Hack, an introductory hacking tutorial to high schoolers around Ann Arbor this Fall at the University of Michigan.

What is Learn to Hack?

    Are you interested in technology? Have you ever found yourself wishing you could create website, apps, and more? Learn to Hack is an event created for high school students, built to overcome the knowledge barrier to coding, to make coding fun, not intimidating. We’re throwing an event for you to do just that.
    This is a fantastic opportunity for you to dive into hacker and maker culture. We’ll be giving detailed tutorials that are led by peers who love to teach, showing you how to construct a fun web application that you can show off to your friends! Through the event, you will gain a solid understanding of procedural programming that you can carry with you regardless of the direction you’re heading.

Learning

Through hacking, you learn what you're truly capable of when faced with a problem. Beyond the technical problem solving skills, you learn how to think quickly and effectively use what you know to learn more.

Bonding

Learn to Hack introduces you to a community of people who are just as excited about building things as you are. You will meet people who may become lifelong friends or even cofounders.

Building

What you build out of this is 100% your own. You'll create a simple application within a webpage, and learn the basics of the languages required to do so.
